<p><p>The nuclear power plant decommissioning market is segmented into three main types: Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR) decommissioning, Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) decommissioning, and others. PWR decommissioning involves dismantling reactors using water under high pressure, while BWR decommissioning deals with reactors that boil water to generate steam directly. "Others" encompasses various reactor types and regions employing distinct decommissioning methods. Each segment has unique regulatory, technical, and economic considerations, influencing overall strategies and timelines for safe decommissioning and waste management.</p></p>
