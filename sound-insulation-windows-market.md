<p><p>The sound insulation windows market primarily features laminated glass and vacuum glass types. Laminated glass comprises layers of glass bonded with a plastic interlayer, effectively reducing noise transmission and enhancing safety. Vacuum glass, on the other hand, consists of two panes separated by a vacuum space, offering superior thermal and acoustic insulation. Both types are increasingly popular in urban areas, where noise pollution is prevalent, as they provide effective solutions for comfort and privacy while improving overall building energy efficiency.</p></p>
