<p><p>The alternating-current transformer market is segmented into three primary categories based on voltage levels. Ultra High Voltage (UHV) transformers operate above 800 kV, enabling the transmission of electricity over vast distances with minimal losses. Extra High Voltage (EHV) transformers, ranging from 345 kV to 800 kV, are essential for interconnecting regional power grids. High Voltage (HV) transformers, operating from 100 kV to 345 kV, are commonly used in industrial and utility applications, facilitating efficient power distribution and management.</p></p>
