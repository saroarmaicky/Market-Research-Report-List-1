<p><ul><li>Contact-based sensing</li><li>Noncontact-based sensing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rotating torque sensors are essential in various industrial applications, and they can be categorized into two main types: contact-based and noncontact-based sensing. Contact-based sensors use mechanical connections to measure torque through deformation, providing high accuracy and reliability. In contrast, noncontact-based sensors utilize techniques like magnetism or electromagnetic fields to measure torque without physical contact, minimizing wear and enabling longer operational lifespans. Each type serves specific needs, offering advantages in different environments, including precision measurement and reduced maintenance.</p></p>
