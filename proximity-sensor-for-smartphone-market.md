<p><p>Proximity sensors for smartphones primarily include optical and ultrasonic displacement sensors. Optical displacement sensors utilize light to detect nearby objects, ensuring that the screen remains off during phone calls when held to the ear, saving battery life. Ultrasonic displacement sensors, on the other hand, emit high-frequency sound waves to measure distance, enabling similar functionality by detecting obstructions and user presence. Both types enhance user experience by preventing accidental screen touches and improving device efficiency during active use.</p></p>
