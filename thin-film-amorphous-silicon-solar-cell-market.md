<p><p>Thin-film amorphous silicon solar cells are categorized into three types based on their junction structures. Single junction cells consist of a single layer of silicon, offering simplicity and lower production costs but limited efficiency. Dual-junction cells stack two layers, enhancing efficiency by capturing a broader spectrum of sunlight. Multi-junction cells combine three or more layers, achieving the highest efficiencies, making them ideal for specialized applications, although they are more complex and expensive to manufacture. Each type serves various market needs.</p></p>
